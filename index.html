Minnesota Shallow-Lake Fish Finder

Minnesota lakes scored for your target species, with an adjustable mean-depth filter. Pick a species and a measure; red = worse, blue = better. Real MN DNR survey data.

What you're looking at

A fishing-scout map of Minnesota's shallow lakes, built entirely from Minnesota DNR fisheries survey data.

Which lakes

Minnesota lakes with DNR survey records for at least one target species (387 lakes statewide). Use the mean-depth slider in the legend to narrow by depth (2–115 ft) — e.g. pull it to 10–25 ft for classic shallow walleye/bass water. The DNR records mean depth only for some lakes; lakes without a recorded mean depth ignore the slider and always appear (their panel notes "depth unknown").

Which fish

Five species only: walleye, muskie, northern pike, largemouth bass, smallmouth bass. Muskie are naturally low-density and sparsely netted, so they appear in only a handful of lakes (often tiny sample sizes).

The three measures

Population density — total fish caught per square mile of lake, from the most recent survey. Lakes are colored by their percentile rank against every other lake for that species, so the middle of the ramp is a typical lake. It reflects survey effort (and small lakes read denser than big ones), so treat it as relative abundance, not a literal headcount.

Biggest fish — average weight for bass & walleye; average length for pike & muskie (the measure anglers actually use for those two).

Pressure trend — the direction of catch rate fitted across multiple surveys: red = declining, blue = improving.

Survey windows — important

This map is built from a static DNR data export whose newest survey is December 2023. The live Minnesota DNR has 2024 and 2025 surveys — they're just not in this downloaded snapshot. Pulling those would require connecting to the DNR's live survey feed (a planned upgrade). So within this snapshot:

• Density & size use each lake's most recent survey from 2019–2023.
• Pressure trend uses all surveys from 2015–2023 (needs 2+ surveys).

A "2023-only" window left just ~15–20 lakes, so the recent window was widened to the last several survey years in the snapshot (most lakes are surveyed every 3–5 years).

Map notes

Lakes are shown as center points sized by surface area, not traced shorelines. Highways/interstates are the generalized Natural Earth network — good for orientation, not every state route.

Sources: MN DNR lake morphology, catch (CPUE), and length-frequency files (static export, newest survey Dec 2023); state boundary & roads from Natural Earth. Live DNR data has newer surveys.
